# How to Submit Knowledge

This project is building a crowdsourced, structured knowledge base.  
You don’t need to be perfect – you just need real experience and honesty.

Right now, all submissions are done through **GitHub Issues**.

---

## Step 1 – Open a New Issue

1. Go to this repository on GitHub.
2. Click the **Issues** tab at the top.
3. Click **New issue**.

---

## Step 2 – Copy the Entry Template

1. Open the file **ENTRY_TEMPLATE.md** in this repo.
2. Click the **Raw** button or select all the text.
3. Copy the entire template.

---

## Step 3 – Fill It Out

1. Paste the template into the **issue description** box.
2. Fill in every section as clearly as you can.
3. Use simple language that a normal person can understand.

You can leave “Contributor Notes” empty if you don’t have any extras to add.

---

## Step 4 – Title and Submit

1. For the **Issue title**, write a short version of the problem or topic.  
   Example: `2007 F-150 rough idle when warm, no check engine light`
2. Click **Submit new issue**.

---

## What Happens Next

- Submissions will be reviewed, cleaned up, and turned into structured entries.
- Over time, approved entries will appear on the public website.
- In the future, contributors may get visible credit and reputation.

Thank you for contributing real-world knowledge.
